# Product

## Register

product

## Users

PKMS is for one primary user, Kenja, using a personal knowledge system across desktop, browser new-tab, terminal, and phone capture contexts. The user context is ADHD-shaped: working memory drops, initiation friction matters more than information availability, and the interface must support episodic return after gaps without shame.

## Product Purpose

PKMS is a hybrid personal knowledge management system where markdown files in `vault/` are the source of truth and SQLite is a regenerable index for search, backlinks, tasks, and resurfacing. It exists to make capture instant, re-entry easy, and old knowledge findable without turning saved material into debt.

Success looks like:

- A thought can be captured in under two seconds with no filing decision.
- Opening a terminal or Firefox new tab restores context with one calm next action.
- Raw captures stay safe in the inbox until folded; the system frames them as progress, never backlog failure.
- Reading, resurfacing, and tasks are rationed into small, recognizable prompts.
- User-visible state lives in markdown/frontmatter, not only in the derived index.

## Brand Personality

Calm, humane, and quietly capable. The product should feel like a trusted desk surface or annotated field notebook: authored and warm, but not cute; structured, but never managerial; honest about what happened, but never accusatory.

## Anti-references

- Momentum-style new tabs with hero clock, generic greeting, photo background, or focus-word theater.
- Productivity dashboards that show raw backlog counts, overdue badges, streaks, red urgency, or "you haven't…" copy.
- SaaS task managers that turn every capture into a to-do list and every saved item into a pile to clear.
- Plugin/settings sprawl that invites perfectionism before use.
- Decorative AI product clichés: generic cream SaaS cards, gradient text, glassmorphism, hero metrics, and identical card grids.

## Design Principles

1. Capture is sacred: zero decisions, instant confirmation, no feed-open side effects.
2. Recognition over recall: show candidates, breadcrumbs, and one next step before asking the user to remember or search.
3. No backlog shame: counts are curated and framed as progress; empty states are rewards.
4. One ambient surface at a time: resurfacing, reading, and tasks are rationed so the front door stays glanceable.
5. Plain files remain the truth: visual state should reveal markdown/frontmatter decisions, not create hidden app-only state.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Design for ADHD first while maintaining general accessibility. Priorities:

- WCAG AA contrast for body text and controls.
- Reduced-motion alternatives for any animation.
- No color-only state communication.
- Keyboard-reachable capture and navigation flows.
- Clear focus states and large-enough click targets.
- Copy that welcomes lapses back instead of billing them.
- Low cognitive load by default, with denser surfaces one click away.
