## RQ1 — Why ADHD users abandon PKM systems

1. **Building the system replaces using it.** The single most-repeated failure mode, across every community and the canonical essays: Obsidian plugin tinkering ([[11-hn]] F1, F2), theme compulsion ([[13-youtube]] F13), "the setup felt great, not the using" ([[12-reddit]] F7), Notion dashboard perfectionism ([[18-landscape]] LS14), "the work becomes infinite, the gains are imaginary" ([[18-landscape]] LS9, LS10). The Obsidian community itself calls the app a toxic-productivity trap and prescribes time-boxing all customization to 2–3 hours total ([[11-hn]] L2-08… filed in [[12-reddit]] L2-08). The user's Notion death is the norm, not an idiosyncrasy ([[14-github]] F17).
2. **Decisions at capture time kill the capture.** "By the time I pick a folder, add tags, set priority… the thing I wanted to write down is just gone" ([[12-reddit]] F4); filing-at-capture is the paralysis trigger ([[12-reddit]] F18, F19; [[11-hn]] F11). Theory agrees: every EF-demanding decision spends the scarcest resource ([[15-barkley]] BK12).
3. **Stale-backlog guilt spirals → bankruptcy.** Ever-growing task lists become "so associated with guilt and stress" they cause paralysis ([[11-hn]] F5); zero-friction capture + zero-friction accumulation = backlog death spiral ([[11-hn]] F6); Anki-style review debt is the same death in SRS form ([[16-academic]] RT6). Shame physiology backs it: shame→freeze→inaction→more shame ([[14-github]] F2; [[16-academic]] MO8).
4. **Systems that require user-maintained invariants collapse on trust.** GTD dies because "I couldn't trust my own brain" to maintain it ([[13-youtube]] F5); written conventions don't govern ADHD behavior — rules competing with immediate reward lose ([[15-barkley]] BK10). A weekly review the user must self-initiate will lapse ([[16-academic]] RT2, RT7).
5. **Novelty decay is the lifecycle, not a defect.** The new-todo-app dopamine fades and users return to baseline ([[11-hn]] F15; [[13-youtube]] F6; [[12-reddit]] L2-07-equiv; [[17-hoarder-mining]] CH14 shows ~2-year tool eras in the user's own saves). Survivors plan for the pivot: data outlives the workflow.
6. **Mobile capture latency is an abandonment cause.** A loading pause at capture was documented as the moment a whole system died ([[18-landscape]] LS14); Notion lost on 10–30s mobile load ([[13-youtube]] F20); org-mode lost 16-year users on the mobile story alone ([[11-hn]] F20).
7. **Tools assume full executive function and get used precisely when it's degraded** ([[11-hn]] F17, 45-year veteran's own failed app). "In a deep low, the system has to get dumber, not smarter" ([[14-github]] F6). Spec against the worst state.

Theory frame for all of it: ADHD is a performance disorder, not a knowledge disorder — storage doesn't change behavior; only what reaches the point of performance does ([[15-barkley]] BK1, BK2).

## RQ2 — What survives >1 year, and why

1. **Zero-friction dumps into surfaces already open.** Telegram Saved Messages, Discord servers, Google Keep, paper stickies ([[12-reddit]] F4, F5; user's own surviving tools per [[00-ground-truths]] B2/B3). First-party proof: saving via one-tap native buttons is the user's only behavior with 4.5 unbroken years of evidence — 55/55 months active ([[17-hoarder-mining]] CH2, CH10).
2. **Daily-note-as-universal-sink, structure derived later.** Logseq journal-first ([[11-hn]] F10), ephemeral daily text files ([[12-reddit]] F8), daily note as extended working memory ([[13-youtube]] F14), 86% of Borretti's nodes were journal entries ([[18-landscape]] LS9, LS13).
3. **Lapse-tolerance beats robustness.** Survivors are cheap to revive: no streaks, no backfill debt, archive-and-restart sanctioned ([[12-reddit]] F7, F12, F24 — bujo survived 5–6 years *because* the method absorbs lapses; the user's own bujo lasted 2.5 years). Design re-entry after dormancy as a first-class flow ([[17-hoarder-mining]] CH4 — his topic engagement is episodic across 6+ years).
4. **Outsourced structure + automated inflow.** Survivors didn't build their systems: paid template + Readwise auto-ingestion ([[13-youtube]] F11); the 2026 wave has the LLM build and maintain the structure ([[13-youtube]] F8, F9; [[11-hn]] F18, F19; [[14-github]] F2–F4). Pre-LLM GitHub has essentially zero ADHD PKM tools — the "markdown vault + agent" wave is the first architecture that structurally fits ([[14-github]] F17).
5. **Mobile capture decides winners** ([[11-hn]] F20; [[13-youtube]] F10) — capture-anywhere outranked data elegance and even data ownership for a diagnosed dev advocate.
6. **Visible state and immediate reward.** Done-lists kept visible ([[12-reddit]] F3), growing link graph as dopamine ([[13-youtube]] F16), green-check feedback ([[12-reddit]] F22), instant closure ([[15-barkley]] BK5, BK11; [[16-academic]] MO9 — delayed payoff is discounted to near zero).
7. **First-party survivors (8 days of hard evidence, [[21-job-search-distill]]):** ⏱/▶/✓ task markers on every task (JS1), decision gates surfaced first (JS2), explicit Icebox (JS3), HANDOFF continuity doc (JS4), dated done-log (JS5), append-only achievement ledger (JS6), sanctioned hyperfocus lane "no guilt, no schedule" (JS7), batched 15-minute unblocks (JS13). Honest exceptions — designed but not sustained: the manually-regenerated kanban PNG (JS8) and practice tasks needing human follow-through (JS14).
8. **Scaffolding is permanent.** Gains persist only while supports persist; prosthetic, not training wheels ([[15-barkley]] BK8; [[16-academic]] SC12). A surviving system is one nobody ever graduates from.

## RQ3 — Frictionless capture

1. **The bar is <2 seconds, zero decisions, cursor already in a textbox.** win+n ([[11-hn]] F4), Drafts opens-to-blank-page ([[19-seed-links]] SD1), the Ilseon "<2s thought-to-saved" budget ([[14-github]] F8), capture-as-interrupt because "an ADHD brain has almost no RAM" ([[13-youtube]] F1).
2. **Capture's psychological product is the security feeling, not future reading.** "Saved, not lost forever, I can move on without guilt" — 267-pt top comment ([[19-seed-links]] SD8; [[11-hn]] F3 "dealt with, asynchronously"; [[12-reddit]] F2). Honor the transaction: instant saved-confirmation, and the *system* owns "later" — a save never re-presented is a broken promise ([[16-academic]] SC3).
3. **The capture surface must not open a feed** ([[13-youtube]] F18) and must not load the full app ([[20-mobile-sync]] MS-06, MS-08; [[14-github]] L2-13).
4. **Duplicates are fine; dedupe is the machine's job** ([[11-hn]] F3). No filing, tagging, or naming at capture ([[15-barkley]] BK12); a "Trash"/no-decision capture folder works in the wild ([[12-reddit]] F13).
5. **Each context needs its own ramp feeding one inbox** — self-regulation doesn't transfer between settings ([[15-barkley]] BK3). Desktop hotkey; Pixel 6 widget/tile (HTTP Shortcuts POST → append-only file in `vault/inbox/`, [[20-mobile-sync]] MS-08, MS-12, MS-13); voice as capture-now-ingest-later ([[20-mobile-sync]] MS-14). The ramp must beat Reddit's save button or it loses ([[17-hoarder-mining]] CH2).
6. **Friction belongs at persistence, not capture.** Zero-friction accumulation is the death spiral; staying alive in the active view should cost a small re-commit (bujo migration; [[11-hn]] F6), and a deliberate "why am I saving this?" template-step is a working filter for read-later queues specifically ([[11-hn]] L2-03) — but only downstream, never on the dump path.
7. **Every capture pays off NOW** — instant confirmation, searchable immediately ([[15-barkley]] BK5; [[16-academic]] MO9). And capture has intrinsic value even at 0% re-open rate — offloading frees working memory ([[19-seed-links]] SD9); never display re-open rates as failure.

## RQ4 — Retrieval, resurfacing, serendipity

1. **Encoding is the deficit; retrieval is intact. Design for recognition, not recall.** Adult-ADHD meta-analysis: memory problems trace to getting information in, not out ([[16-academic]] RT3); show candidates and let the user say "that one" — a blank search box demands the free-recall they're worst at (RT4). Community mirror: associative link-following, never location-recall ([[13-youtube]] F15); tag entropy defeats tag systems ([[12-reddit]] F16).
2. **Event/context cues, not clocks.** Time-based prospective memory is severely impaired while event-based is intact ([[16-academic]] RT2); anchor resurfacing to moments — opening a project, creating the daily note — not to "review at 9am Friday" (SC9; [[15-barkley]] BK2 point-of-performance).
3. **Resurfacing is pushed, machine-initiated, and shaped as a curious question** — "Still interested in GPU benchmarks?" — never a count badge ([[14-github]] F9, F10). Passive piles are already solved and already fail ([[12-reddit]] F1, F2); write-only graveyards are the default outcome unless resurfacing is first-class ([[11-hn]] F13, F14).
4. **Ambient related-notes is the cheapest high-value serendipity** — local embeddings, zero user actions ([[18-landscape]] LS6). Rare rediscovery joy is the reinforcement loop that sustains the habit ([[19-seed-links]] SD10); bounded variable-reward "surprise me" resurfacing taps it deliberately ([[16-academic]] RT12, flagged as extrapolation).
5. **Notifications are a depleting currency.** Acceptance drops ~30% per repeated alert ([[16-academic]] SC8); 20 triggers become wallpaper ([[19-seed-links]] SD5). Ration to one ambient surface; vary form; never repeat unchanged.
6. **Re-entry beats organization.** The killer cost is "Monday with zero memory of Friday" ([[12-reddit]] F6); resumption cues at the breakpoint sharply cut resume lag ([[16-academic]] RT10, RT9 context reinstatement); breadcrumbs-with-dead-ends, state restore, timeline ([[14-github]] F16); Captain's Log / `_why.md` re-read each session ([[11-hn]] F9; [[14-github]] F11). First-party: HANDOFF.md with "suggested first action" actively maintained ([[21-job-search-distill]] JS4).
7. **Visible aging, then amnesty** — ambient staleness marks beat hidden timestamps ([[11-hn]] F8), untouched items sink out of default views rather than accusing ([[11-hn]] F14; [[12-reddit]] F21). Review-queue debt must be structurally impossible: missed days decay silently, no overdue counters ([[16-academic]] RT6, RT7).
8. **OCR/extract text from image captures at ingest** or they're permanently unsearchable ([[19-seed-links]] SD7) — the user's meme-screenshot habit is a live specimen.

## RQ5 — The 90%-done problem and shame-free note rot

1. **Notes and tasks need opposite decay policies.** Rediscovering a 5-year-old idea is gold; a 5-year-old undone task is poison ([[11-hn]] F5). Knowledge persists and resurfaces; tasks expire with built-in amnesty (bujo's blank page) unless re-committed.
2. **Starting is success; completion is optional.** Store a concrete first step WITH the capture ([[14-github]] F7; [[11-hn]] L2-06 — incompleteness, not difficulty, triggers indefinite deferral); the user double-saved "if it's worth doing, it's worth doing poorly" ([[17-hoarder-mining]] CH8); graded tiny re-entry rungs have real evidence ([[16-academic]] MO11; [[15-barkley]] BK11 step-a-day).
3. **One next action; the backlog stays invisible.** "You never see the whole list" ([[14-github]] F6); never enumerate all steps — full decomposition makes projects feel infinite ([[13-youtube]] F7); Done/Stuck/Not-now grammar, Not-now guilt-free ([[19-seed-links]] SD12); first-party: one-task-at-a-time preamble, Icebox, sanctioned hyperfocus lane ([[21-job-search-distill]] JS3, JS7).
4. **Paused is a first-class state with a reactivation condition** ([[13-youtube]] F12; [[21-job-search-distill]] JS9 — "APPLY ENGINE PAUSED… the day that decision flips to go"). Pause over delete.
5. **The machine tracks WIP.** #wip + mtime queries delegate 90%-done bookkeeping ([[12-reddit]] F13); staleness detection + gentle resurfacing + a scheduled "good enough to ship?" perfectionism interrupt ([[14-github]] F9).
6. **Log done things, including retroactively.** Add-then-check-off converts invisible work into closure ([[11-hn]] F7); writing "done" entries unsticks overwhelm ([[12-reddit]] F14); the dated done-log and one-sitting unblock batches are demonstrably used first-party ([[21-job-search-distill]] JS5, JS13).
7. **Shame is a design input, not a tone preference.** Shame-freeze loop named as the core mechanism to break, derived from 100+ real sessions ([[14-github]] F2); measurably lower self-compassion mediates worse outcomes ([[16-academic]] MO8); anything reading as criticism drives quiet abandonment (MO7, flagged). No guilt language, no streaks, briefings end "with an invitation, not an assignment" ([[14-github]] F3); gaps are welcomed back, not billed ([[12-reddit]] L2-09). Near-in-time accountability still matters — low-consequence environments produce nothing ([[17-hoarder-mining]] CH7) — but it's consequence-proximity, not shame.
8. **Notes are permanently-in-progress pages, not finishable artifacts** (gwern's long-content framing, [[18-landscape]] coverage notes) — removes the "finish the note" frame entirely.

## RQ6 — Triage inbox ⇄ career ops ⇄ vault

1. **Two-stage architecture: transient triage inbox → persistent vault, with an explicit promote-or-purge gate.** The cleanest answer found, repeated independently ([[14-github]] F8; [[11-hn]] F12; [[12-reddit]] F18). Different friction budgets per tier: many-times-daily surfaces near-zero, weekly reference stores can be heavier ([[12-reddit]] F17).
2. **content-hoarder IS the inbox, and it's unprocessable by design: 97.55% of 84,250 items never left 'inbox'.** Per-item review has never happened and never will ([[17-hoarder-mining]] CH1). Design promote-on-demand (search/pull) + guilt-free bulk decay; never a review queue. ~80% of the hoard is entertainment needing a source-level prefilter so triage attention is never spent on it (CH11).
3. **The win scenario is half-built.** content-hoarder already stores full Reddit post+comment-tree JSON (672 threads hydrated; 9,593 individual comments saved — [[17-hoarder-mining]] CH6); a community archiver doing exactly this exists ([[12-reddit]] F20). Don't rebuild capture — build the promote pipeline that renders a thread to markdown in the vault at promote time. The archive, not Reddit, is the system of record: native saves silently cap at ~1000 ([[12-reddit]] F20) and services die — Pocket is dead ([[18-landscape]] LS8). Store full content locally at save time.
4. **LLM triage grammar: dump → agent classifies/proposes → human picks.** ADHD users are "bad at generating the ideal plan, extremely proficient at identifying the best from a list" ([[12-reddit]] F23, F9); present-then-ask, one question at a time, never a blank prompt ([[19-seed-links]] SD6); swipe-grade per-item triage cost ([[19-seed-links]] SD11); auto-summarize-into-daily-note pipelines already work in the wild ([[13-youtube]] F9). Caution: automation must not silently grow a bigger look-at-later pile — bound and surface the queue, the ritual of emptying matters ([[11-hn]] F11; [[19-seed-links]] SD3 — trust requires scheduled consumption).
5. **Career ops stays a separate structured workspace.** The hoard can't feed it — job-search saves are diffuse news/memes, no playbooks ([[17-hoarder-mining]] CH12). job-search-2026 already works; the vault relates to it via promoted references and shared conventions (⏱▶✓, HANDOFF, ledger — [[21-job-search-distill]]), not absorption. The briefing-package pattern (context+guardrails+task spec+output location) is the user's native multi-agent idiom (JS11) — reuse it for triage agents.
6. **Extraction beats hoarding distillations.** The user hoards 131-tip megaposts and 36-app reviews that sit unprocessed ([[17-hoarder-mining]] CH5) — compilations need an extraction step into small resurfaceable notes or they become the deepest-buried items of all.

## Cross-cutting architecture verdicts

- **LLM-as-organizer is the first structurally viable ADHD PKM architecture** ([[14-github]] F17) — and the surviving shape is **AI as a regenerable layer over plain files** (Smart Connections, Obsidian Copilot, basic-memory — all active), while **AI-as-app dies** (Reor archived, Khoj pivoted) ([[18-landscape]] LS2–LS6). Mirrors "file over app" (LS12) and validates the markdown-vault + derived-SQLite scaffold.
- **Mobile architecture: reuse content-hoarder's proven pattern** — vault and index stay on Windows; capture + triage reach the Pixel 6 as a tailnet-private HTTPS PWA (`tailscale serve`), with HTTP-Shortcuts one-tap POST capture into append-only inbox files; file sync (Syncthing fork) optional and never load-bearing; git-on-phone ruled out ([[20-mobile-sync]] MS-01, MS-08, MS-10–MS-13).
- **The system is the prosthesis and it never comes off** ([[15-barkley]] BK8). Externalize all three: working memory (capture + resurfacing), time (countdowns, visible age, escalating salience — dates-as-metadata are invisible, BK4), and motivation (immediate payoffs, BK7). Encode rules as environment, not documentation (BK10).
- **Cap the customization surface deliberately.** Every knob is a perfectionism trap for this profile ([[11-hn]] F1; [[13-youtube]] F13; [[18-landscape]] LS9); ship opinionated and finished-feeling; let the novelty itch be scratched at the view layer while the data substrate stays boring ([[11-hn]] F15). Anchor every feature to a named context of use or cut it ([[18-landscape]] LS10).

## Contested / open questions (carry into Phase 3)

- **Gamification durability for an adult engineer** — evidence is pediatric, modest (g≈0.28), and hints at age-decay ([[16-academic]] MO10); completions/progress-bars survive while points/streaks decay ([[13-youtube]] F4). Lean on intrinsic loop, decorate lightly.
- **Interest-based nervous system & RSD** — clinically resonant, formally unvalidated ([[16-academic]] MO6, MO7). Use as heuristics to test against the user's own reactions, not laws.
- **Self-imposed urgency gets rejected** ("you made that up", [[13-youtube]] F3) vs. near-in-time accountability demonstrably saved by the user ([[17-hoarder-mining]] CH7) — the line between fake deadlines and real consequence-proximity needs a Phase 3 decision.
- **PWA-only vs PWA+sync** for the phone: does the user need offline whole-vault read, or capture-offline + read-over-tailnet ([[20-mobile-sync]] open questions)?
- **Where the capture endpoint lives** — inside content-hoarder's Flask app or a sibling service ([[20-mobile-sync]]).
- **Show-candidates vs search-box** has mechanism-level support but no direct ADHD study ([[16-academic]] RT3/RT4) — cheap in-product A/B later.
- Quote-discipline note: motivation-track blockquotes in [[16-academic]] failed verbatim verification (claims held; quotes were paraphrases) — re-check before re-quoting anywhere user-facing.
